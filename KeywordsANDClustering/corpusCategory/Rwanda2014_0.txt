Rwanda has experienced strong inclusive growth since 2000 . Real GDP growth has averaged 7.8 percent in a broadly stable economic environment. Poverty declined from about 60 percent in 2000 to below 45 percent in 2010/11, and the poorest have benefited the most from growth. Inclusive growth has been led by policies geared towards agriculture through investments in fertilizers, improved seeds, electrification, irrigation, and rural roads and better provision of social services, especially to the rural poor.
