Implementing reforms and gradually removing policy accommodation will be key to sustaining the growth momentum with macrofinancial stability. A recalibration of policy settings, including the removal of monetary accommodation, medium term fiscal consolidation, and a tightening of macroprudential measures to tame the credit cycle is appropriate to prevent the building of cyclical imbalances. Ensuring sustainable and faster potential growth would require a continued impetus on structural reforms to address infrastructure bottlenecks and improve the business climate.
